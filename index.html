Vizualizing American Urbanization trends

Gautam Sarkar | gsarkar@ucsc.edu | Github link

The purpose of this is to see how American cities grow in comparison to cities in the rest of the world over time. There are a number of avenues I can see this project taking from having a side-by-side comparison tool to look at two cities land area at user-specified times from the Atlas for urban expansion dataset to having a Hans Rosling Gapminder style scatterplot over time of cities' populations or, if possible, population density or something even more advanced. If there is very interesting, detailed data for the U.S. and not Europe I am fine with just vizualizing U.S. cities.

Data sources:

  • Atlas for urban expansion
  • U.N. World Urbanization Prospects
  • U.S. Census(probably will not use)
  • Centre for Cities European Cities Data Tool(probably will not use)

Vizualization sources:

  • Cities land area change in last 200 years(animated)
  • Comparing current cities with Atlanta

  • Density of housing developed in various U.S. cities by decade(animated)
  • World City Populations over time
