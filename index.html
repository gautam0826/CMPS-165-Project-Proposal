The Effect of (American) Urbanization on the Environment

Gautam Sarkar | gsarkar@ucsc.edu | Github link

The purpose of this is to see how sprawl affects cities' air pollution. There are two tracks we are considering: using cities across the world or using more fine-grined U.S. city data. Currently we have data on cities around the world's air quality for the last decade, with only one value present for most cities not allowing for seeing pollution change over time and the associated Atlas for urban expansion data contains a ton of factors like (Built-up Area, Urban Built-up Aren, Suburban Built-up Area, Urban Extent Population, etc.) for three time periods: ~1990, ~2000, and ~2013. The U.S. EPA data contains metrics for various pollutants from 1990 for hundreds of cities year-by-year and Census data is available for 1990, 2000, and 2010 including cities' population density and for 2000 and 2010 additional factors like land area and housing units. A minor problem would be the number of different pollutants used and metrics but the sheer amount of cities makes up for it.
